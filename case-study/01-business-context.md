# Executive Summary

Nova Retail Group is a fictional international retailer generating thousands of sales transactions every day through online and in-store channels. As the business grows, decision-makers need reliable, centralized analytics to monitor performance, understand customer behavior, and support strategic planning.

This case study demonstrates how **Microsoft Fabric** can be used to build a unified analytics platform that transforms raw transactional data into trusted business insights.

---

# Business Context

The company relies on transactional sales data to evaluate business performance, identify customer trends, and measure product success. However, raw operational data alone cannot support decision-making without a structured analytics platform.

To address this need, the organization decided to adopt **Microsoft Fabric**, leveraging its unified analytics capabilities to simplify data engineering, semantic modeling, and business intelligence within a single environment.

---

# Business Objectives

The platform is designed to help business users:

* Monitor sales performance.
* Analyze revenue trends over time.
* Identify top-performing products.
* Understand customer purchasing behavior.
* Deliver trusted KPIs through a centralized analytics platform.
* Support strategic business decisions with reliable data.

---

# Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if it delivers:

* A unified Lakehouse architecture.
* Reliable and reusable analytical datasets.
* Automated data transformation workflows.
* Consistent business KPIs.
* Interactive Power BI reporting.
* A scalable Microsoft Fabric analytics platform.
