# Executive Summary

As Nova Retail Group expands, the volume of transactional data continues to grow. While this data contains valuable business insights, extracting meaningful information becomes increasingly difficult without a modern analytics platform.

This document outlines the key business and technical challenges that motivated the implementation of a unified analytics solution.

---

# Current Challenges

The organization faces several challenges that limit its ability to transform operational data into reliable business insights.

### Data Quality

Raw transactional data contains missing values, inconsistent formats, duplicate records, and business anomalies that reduce reporting accuracy.

### Fragmented Analytics

Business data is stored in operational datasets that are not optimized for analytical workloads, making reporting slow and difficult to maintain.

### Inconsistent Business Metrics

Without a centralized business model, different teams may calculate KPIs differently, leading to inconsistent reporting and reduced confidence in business decisions.

### Manual Data Preparation

Preparing analytical datasets requires repetitive manual transformations, increasing both processing time and the risk of human error.

---

# Business Requirements

To overcome these challenges, the future analytics platform must:

* Centralize analytical data in a single platform.
* Automate data cleansing and transformation.
* Standardize business metrics across reports.
* Support scalable analytical workloads.
* Provide trusted datasets for business users.
* Simplify collaboration between technical and business teams.

---

# Expected Outcome

The organization requires an analytics platform capable of transforming raw transactional data into reliable, governed, and business-ready information while reducing operational complexity.

These requirements naturally lead to the evaluation of **Microsoft Fabric** as a unified analytics platform capable of supporting the complete data lifecycle.
